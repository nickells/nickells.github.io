About

Hello! I am Nick Ellsworth, and this is my online portfolio. Welcome. Take a look around. Stop and smell the DOM tree.

After completing my music major in Clarinet Performance, I enrolled in Fullstack Academy to continue nurturing my need to build and create. I spent three months in their intensive course, learning the ins and outs of MEAN stack Javascript. Afterwards, I was selected to join the Fullstack team for a Fellowship position, which is part teaching-assistant and part software engineer. I spent another 13 weeks there, gaining valuable experience mentoring others and working on an Agile team. In February 2016, I started doing web development at re#, a production and development consultancy based in Brooklyn.

Projects

I have built several projects since the beginning of my programming career. Several of these were created during my time at Fullstack. (This list does not contain work-related projects)

tiny-interpolator

I wanted an easy way to update the data on my personal website without digging through HTML every time. This is an npm module that lets you inject data into a template and render on the client side. It was made with the intent of being as simple and small as possible, with the minified version coming in at just under 1 Kb.

Breezeblocks

Built with Angular, this app gives users an intuitive drag-and-drop UI for prototyping React Native apps. Layout is converted into a JSON object and sent to the backend, where it is converted into JSX. This code is then uploaded to its own Github repo! This team project was built in fulfillment of the Fullstack Academy curriculum.

Dictatone

Allows a user to sing, hum, or whistle into their microphone and see, magically, their melodies transcribed into printable sheet music. Won a "Mad Scientist" hackathon award at Fullstack. Also built in fulfillment of the Fullstack Academy curriculum.

MementoDash

Loosely based off of Momentum Dashboard, this is a "User Dashboard" which takes images from the "SFWPorn" series on Reddit. Categories are chosen on the right side of the screen and images are accessed through the Imgur API. Built in Angular.

BlipCrowd

Creating a music sequencer was always something I'd had in mind since my very first-for loop. This one is unique because it uses websockets, meaning that anyone anywhere can collaborate on the same sequencer board simultaneously. (Also it's chromatic, while most are pentatonic. Personal preference.)

Toys

Often I have a need for something small or silly, which might be useful but not on the scale of a larger endeavor.

Keyboard Simulator 2015

A little app/experiment stemming from my admirance of mechanical keyboards that plays clicky sounds with every keydown and keyup event, and shows a neat custom CSS keyboard animation that maps to each key you press. Oddly satisfying to watch and listen to. Guaranteed at least 5 minutes of entertainment.

clebcraft

My friend made a Minecraft server and I wanted an easy way to see who was online and other server statistics. This is a small, responsive app built in Angular that polls the Minecraft server every 5 seconds for information.

Emoji Converter for Slack

After a breakthrough one productive afternoon at work, I realized I could make ASCII art with Slack emojis. Subsequently, I automated this process.

Finger Trainer

Once I acquired a featureless Das Keyboard, I realized I was really bad at touch-typing the top row of keys. So I made this tool to help me.
