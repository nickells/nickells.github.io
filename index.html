About

Hello! I am Nick Ellsworth, and you seem to have found my online portfolio. Welcome. Take a look around. Stop and smell the DOM tree. Pleasant, right?

After completing my music major in Clarinet Performance, I enrolled in Fullstack Academy to continue nurturing my need to build and create. I spent three months in their intensive course, learning the ins and outs of MEAN stack Javascript. It was quite the whirlwind. Afterwards, I joined the Fullstack team for a "Fellowship" position, which is essentially part teaching-instructor and part software engineer. After another 13 weeks, I've gained valuable experience mentoring others and working on an Agile team. Currently on the job hunt.

Projects

I have built several projects since the beginning of my programming career. They range from silly to serious, and annoying to useful. Several of these were created during my time at Fullstack.

Breezeblocks

Built with Angular, this app gives users an intuitive drag-and-drop UI for prototyping React Native apps. Layout is converted into a JSON object and sent to the backend, where it is converted into JSX. This code is then uploaded to its own Github repo, ready to download and compile with Xcode! This team project was built in fulfillment of the Fullstack Academy curriculum.

Dictatone

Allows a user to sing, hum, or whistle into their microphone and see, magically, their melodies transcribed into printable sheet music. Won a "Mad Scientist" hackathon award at Fullstack. Also built in fulfillment of the Fullstack Academy curriculum.

MementoDash

Based off Momentum Dash, this is a "User Dashboard" which takes images from the "SFWPorn" series on Reddit. Categories are chosen on the right side of the screen and images are accessed through the Imgur API. Built in Angular.

BlipCrowd

Creating a music sequencer was always something I'd had in mind since my very first-for loop. This one is unique because it uses websockets, meaning that anyone anywhere can collaborate on the same sequencer board simultaneously. (Also it's chromatic, while most are pentatonic. Personal preference.)

Keyboard Simulator 2015

A silly little app, it plays clicky sounds with every keydown and keyup event, and shows a neat keyboard animation that maps to each key you press. Oddly satisfying to watch and listen to. Guaranteed at least 5 minutes of entertainment.

Emoji Converter for Slack

After a breakthrough one productive afternoon at work, I realized I could make ASCII art with Slack emojis. Subsequently, I automated this process.
